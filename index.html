History of Nashville

The first ones to settle in the area now known as Nashville were Native Americans of Mississippian culture followed by the Cherokee, Chickasaw, and the Shawnee. Nashville was first established in 1779. The city was also named after a Continental Army general named Francis Nash. The city was quickly gaining popularity because there were new opportunities and jobs available. Since Nashville had a lot of popularity, Nashville was officially named the capital of Tennessee in 1843 beating Charlotte, Tennessee for the title. Nashville became known as 'Music City' because many muscicians flocked to Nashville in seek to get a chance to perform on one of the many stages in the city, making Nashville become known for music. Nashville's History Fun Facts

  • Nashville was first named Nashborough until the named was changed to Nashville in 1784.
  • Nashville was founded by James Robertson and a group of Wataugans in 1779.
  • Nashville became the first city in the U.S. to form a metropolitan government.

Culture of Nashville

The culture here in Nashville is very lively. It is filled with music, art, and even knowledge!

  1. Before Nashville was coined as 'Music City', it was known as the 'Athens of the South' because of the amount of colleges in the city and because Nashville was the first city in the South to implement a public school system.
  2. For the art portion, Nashville has many art galleries. The center of art in Nashville is the Frist Art Museum. A very popular art event in Nashville is called the First Saturday Art Crawl. It is a free event where galleries all over the city come together to show their art.
  3. It is obvious by now, but Nashville is filled with music. There are many centers that show off Nashville's mucical talent. One of those centers are called Tennessee Performing Arts Center. There you could watch all of the Broadway shows to your heart's content. Nashville even has their own Nashville Symphony who won a Grammy! From Country to Broadway to even Classical music, Nashville has it all!
